Architecture and Art of Sri Lanka



   Sri Lanka's architecture showcases a wide range of architectural forms and styles. Since Buddhism was brought to the island in the third century BCE, it has had a considerable impact on Sri Lankan architecture.


   However, Sri Lanka's structure also reflects practices and styles that were formed in Europe and Asia.


   A lot of Sri Lankan trades and crafts are influenced by the country's long-standing Buddhist culture, which has in turn absorbed and adopted a lot of native and original traditions. Most often, religious ideas are the inspiration for Sri Lankan art, which can take many different forms like shape, oil, and building. Grottoes and tabernacle oils, which resemble the ones exhibited at Sigiriya as well as the religious oils exhibited in tabernacles at Dambulla and the Temple of the Tooth Relic in Kandy, are among the most famous features of Sri Lankan art. Both natives and foreign settlers have described other well-liked forms of art.


   As an example, the hill country is home to traditional rustic crafts and beautiful crockery, while Batik and lacework with Portuguese and Indonesian influences have become well-known.


   It features a wide variety of lovely delineations. The performing arts are adored in Sri Lanka. Bollywood is the main performance aesthetic. Buddhism and early Portuguese painters are the two main sources of influence on Sri Lankan music. Buddhism arrived in Sri Lanka after the Buddha's visit there in 300 BC, and the Portuguese arrived there in the 15th century, bringing with them cantiga ditties, the ukulele, and guitars, as well as African slaves who helped to diversify the island's musical heritage. The cotillion music played by these slaves was known as baila and they were known as kaffrinha. Drumming was and still is a vital component of music in Sri Lanka's Buddhist and Hindu temples, which is included in the narcotic Kandyan cans-style music that is traditionally played. Western dancing and music are most prevalent in Sri Lanka's western corridor.
